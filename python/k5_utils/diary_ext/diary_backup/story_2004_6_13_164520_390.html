what a match (none / 2) (#12)
by Timo Laine on Sun Jun 13th, 2004 at 06:17:10 PM EST
http://www.timoroso.com/

This was essentially boring soccer most of the time, with only three highlights: England's goal, the save from Beckham's penalty by Barthez and Zidane's goal from the freekick. The single big mistake in the game was the unbelievable blunder by the Gerrard-James duo that resulted in the penalty for France.

I don't think either team deserved to win this one, but England lost only because of their own mistakes. Beckham had the chance to clinch the match with his penalty. England couldn't stop Zidane's brilliant freekick, though, but there is no reason they couldn't have secured a draw.

I was pretty neutral about the match myself. But I don't like ultra-defensive tactics, even when they are effective, as Eriksson's tactics were (at least initially). I don't like bad finishing either, and I expected more from Henry and Trezeguet. But I am still quite happy that even if most of the match was pretty boring, at least there was drama. It reminded me a bit of the Champions League final with Bayern Munich and Manchester United and maybe also of the Euro 2000 final. This was not a classic match, but I think it's still an example of why it is so easy to be passionate about the sport. Your squad can work like a clockwork for 99% of the match, and you can still lose. That is utterly, totally and without question unfair, and it is also one of the reasons why this is more than a sport, and perhaps more than life itself for many people.
