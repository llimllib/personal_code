Diary of a debugger (Diaries)

By llimllib
Mon Sep 29th, 2003 at 07:31:16 AM EST

Been working on an algorithms assignment for about 14 hours straight now.

music: Skatalites - Rough & Tough

4:45: I have to write a program implementing a simulated printer queue. I spent about 4 hours on it yesterday, and I'm on about hour 14 today. This program has been a bastard from start to finish, and I need to have it done in 3.5 hours. I have to do it in MSVC, which sucks. My program keeps b0rking when I switch from debug compilation to -O2.
Fiona Apple - Paper bag

debugging...

4:57: fixed that one, had a long overflow, switched to unsigned long, that seemed to work. Now, however, it seems that my queue never empties.
Pink Floyd - Money

debugging...

5:08: alright, the queue is sometimes empty, and I'm getting reasonable average times, urgent prints go faster than normal ones. The queue never seems to be full, so I've kicked it up to some higher numbers, which leaves me more time to type while I wait for the days to simulate.
Black Star - B Boys Will B Boys

debugging...

5:26: Fucked if I know. Behavior seems random. When I start my search at a high number of jobs per day, the queue is never empty, even as the number decreases. When I start it at a low number, search goes much faster, but the queue is never full, even when they reach the same number. arrggggggh. It's cold outside.
Rage Against The Machine - Guerilla Radio

debugging...

5:43: hands shaking. tired. Considered replacing my queue implementation, decided against it. Not even sure what's messed up. Giving up isn't an option anymore, two hours of sleep would just be worse than not sleeping until I'm done with my classes. Definitely skipping work in the math help room this afternoon.
Lovage - Everyone has a Summer

debugging...

6:05: Wow, breakthrough. I'm still in bad physical shape, my head seems to drift off from my body every once in a while, I'm actually surprised I was coherent enough to make a breakthrough. I wasn't reinitializing the probability that a job will be printed (jobs per day / cycles per workday) when I started a new simulation. This means I've effectively been running and rerunning the same godawful simulation for the past hour, and why the simulation was different if I started it at 4000 jobs per day vs. 10000. Still no sunlight, I'm surprised.
Muddy Waters - Sad Sad day

debugging...

6:50: Sunlight, code improving, documentation being done. I also improved a tricky algorithm by a lot, I think. I have to print this out in 40 minutes if I go to the class, 1:40 if I don't. work.
Beethoven - 6th Symphony (unknown orchestra)

debugging...

7:29: OK, it works. my new algorithm works about 100 times faster than the previous one. Once morning came, I felt a remarkable clarity. Unfortunately, I don't think I have enough time to finish documenting the code and go to class, so I'll be working on his code while he's giving class. My professor doesn't really care, fortunately. I can't wait to get real food.
People Under the Stairs - Acid Raindrops

Sleep deprivation -> bad code (5.00 / 2) (#3)
by swr on Mon Sep 29th, 2003 at 08:01:48 AM EST

Working into the wee hours of the morning is usually a bad idea.

Once you've slept, you'll probably realize your new algorithm that is 100 times faster has some fatal flaw. That happens pretty often when doing the all-nighter thing. The "extra" time you spend working on the code usually translates into roughly 2x that time in debugging.

In my experience, the only way to reliably get away with an all-nighter is to have the critical stuff finished by 2 or 3 am, then the rest (until work/school starts the next morning) is minor fixes, tweaks, and polish. Trivial stuff is the most one can can reasonably expect to accomplish when sleep deprived.

In general though, I agree that it's better to *try* and avoid such binges, as they do tend to produce shitty code, although they produce a lot of code. As always, it's a tradeoff.
