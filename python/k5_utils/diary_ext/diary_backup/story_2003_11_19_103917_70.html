Sure, I'll do a diary (Diaries)

By llimllib
Wed Nov 19th, 2003 at 10:39:17 AM EST

I mean, since nobody else is writing an interesting diary that I can leave comments in, why don't I write one? I guess that means I'll have to be interesting then. Let's see...interesting...interesting.

Thoughts:

  • Why are there only about 4 diaries this morning?

  • ESR is a high IQ snob, to put it mildly.

  • I was my brother's sponsor for confirmation this weekend. The Bishop performed the service, and he mentioned meditation and Thomas Merton in his homily, which surprised me. He was the first person I'd describe as intellectual that I've ever met associated with the church. I talked to him afterwards, he seemed quite affable, well read, and well rounded. I wish I had some time to really pick his brains.

  • Kasparov drew against X3D Fritz. He played a brilliant game 3 to follow up a very poor game 2 in which Fritz even showed a bit of strategic legs. games 1 and 4 were drawn, and I haven't examined either. From my perspective, the most interesting bits of the tournament came in game 3, when Fritz just could not play to its advantage because its strategy lay more than 20 moves down the road. Instead of playing like it should have, it lay back and did nothing, allowing Kasparov to set up his pieces just like he wanted. Even when Kasparov had a huge advantage, Fritz only calculated a small one.

    Perhaps somebody will write an MLP on this tournament?

  • Designing web pages sucks and is boring. I always forget this and agree to do people's webpages. Stupid me.

  • Is this interesting yet?

  • SquirrelMail seems to have a bug, as it is truncating a c++ file I attached to an email. The file is definitely all there in the mailbox, but squirrelmail won't let the whole thing be downloaded. This is a pain, since I can't print from my machine. Stupid Netware.

  • B-Tree deletion is hard.

  • The UConn Huskies are going to win the national championship (men's college basketball, for the heathens out there). By the end of the season, Emeka Okafor will be absolutely unstoppable, and will force other teams out of the lane at will. As a Connecticut product, that's the only interesting sport we have in the state, so we're all crazy about UConn. Should be a good year. Oh, and they beat Yale but had a moderately tough time. Early season jitters, I say.

  • Surely you've found something to comment on here, so do your thing.
