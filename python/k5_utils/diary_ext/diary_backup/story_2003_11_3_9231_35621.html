Pics of Chicks with Specs! (Diaries)

By llimllib
Mon Nov 3rd, 2003 at 09:23:01 AM EST

Yeah, so, for my halloween costume, I was a geek. A stretch, I know, but I look a little too convincing. Does anyone know where you can still buy pocket protectors? That would have really rounded out my costume nicely. Unfortunately, when I went to the senior party on Saturday, I found that a whole bunch of girls also dressed as geeks. Disappointing.

But, I digress. Friday night I walked around wearing the glasses on top of regular clothes. On a whim, I decided to have nearly every girl I know, and some I don't, put on the glasses so I could take pictures. Believe it or not, there are more than 2 pictures.

When I left the thrift store (on Friday afternoon, naturally), I intended to be Stuart Smalley. I had bought a cardigan, a really ugly shirt, some khakis I could hike up, and some plaid grey socks. I bought the glasses because I couldn't remember if Franken wore his glasses when he played Smalley. However, when I got home, I found that my cardigan and shirt didn't really match Smalley's, who also didn't wear glasses, so I opted for the ease of the geek costume.

Friday night, I didn't feel like wearing my costume, and I just put on the glasses on a whim. I didn't really have anything planned for the evening, so I put my beer in a coffee mug (we don't get harassed if we do that - my school's liberal about alcohol) and thought I'd see what parties were going on. My friends downstairs were having a party with a different fraternity than mine, but I knew the party hosts (people who can't drink and are supposed to control the party), so I hung out outside with them for a while. Soon, my suite was hopping and their party had opened to the public, so I had a fine time drinking and flirting.

(I'm in a very parenthetical mood today)

Saturday night was the night of the senior class halloween party, which I had heard is one of the best parties around. Naturally, I was in attendance, this time in full costume. My buddy Jeff went as only the finest of women, and Bryan is in the background in his militia (too lazy to actually dress up) gear. Other excellent costumes were in attendance. A group of Peanuts characters took home one group prize, and an oompa-loompa and various other charlie and the chocolate factory characters took the other. The girls got mad when the individual costume winner went to a girl basically dressed in a body stocking with about 3 leaves glued to it (I was OK with the situation).

I was not in a particularly good mood Saturday, hence no pictures of the winners, or even of me with many girls. You ever feel that some nights you just don't have your game? I just couldn't get comfortable on saturday night, and spent most of it drinking whiskey and cokes from the open bar.
