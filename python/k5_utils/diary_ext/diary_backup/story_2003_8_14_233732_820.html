We Need Diary Ratings + Programming mental block (Diaries)

By llimllib
Thu Aug 14th, 2003 at 11:37:32 PM EST

We really need diary ratings. Also, frisbee tournament this weekend and my programming impotence.

Part Numbered 1:

We really should have diary ratings. Seeing splitpeasoup's diary containing the top 3 diaries of the day reminded me of this, when it allowed me to see DU's excellent diary about playing go against a badly aging mathematician. All I would want to see would be a list of all the diaries of the past 24 hours, in order of their ranking.

I would write up a rant about this and post it as meta, but it appears to me that nobody has any power to change this site right now, so it would accomplish nothing.

Part Numero 2:

My team is going to play in the finals of our frisbee league this weekend. Last night, we won our conference championships, making us 1 of the top 2 seeds out of 64 teams. Winning is awesome.

Part 3 Number:

I have been totally unable to program for the past 3 weeks or so. Not sure if I'm depressed, burned out, or just psyching myself out, but damned if I can even open a file of code for more than a few minutes. I have a couple of interesting projects to work on, so it's not a problem of inspiration. Any suggestions? What do you do when you feel this way?

Programmer's block (5.00 / 1) (#9)
by KWillets on Fri Aug 15th, 2003 at 01:36:58 AM EST
(kendall@same-as-homepage.org) http://willets.org

I see programming as a means to an end.  If the end isn't exciting enough, I won't do it.  

However, I've been diddling around with other issues for weeks now, even though I have something I really would like to get working.  So far I've narrowed the problems down to concerns about protecting what I write, and the need to specify what I want to do very clearly before I start.  

For the latter issue, it often works to make diagrams of data structures, or write pseudo code, which is much more attuned to finding and fixing mistakes than actual code.  

I've also found that the less focused I am on something, the more I have to document it.  Right now I'm writing pages and pages on how to manage a B-tree, which is about 90% standard algorithms, and 10% strange new features.
